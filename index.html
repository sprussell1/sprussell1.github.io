Simple, consistent habits are the best defense against common illnesses. Teaching these practices to children can significantly reduce their risk of getting sick and spreading germs to others.

A Closer Look: Illness Details

Respiratory Illnesses

Symptoms:

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat and sneezing
  • Cough, mild headache, or body aches

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets and direct contact with infected secretions or surfaces.

Incubation Period:

1 to 3 days.

Contagious Period:

From a day or two before symptoms start until all symptoms are gone (often 7-10 days).

Return to School:

When they feel well enough to participate. No fever for 24 hours.

Symptoms:

  • Sudden fever & chills
  • Cough & sore throat
  • Body aches & fatigue

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from coughing, sneezing, or talking.

Incubation Period:

1 to 4 days.

Contagious Period:

From 1 day before symptoms until 5-7 days after.

Return to School:

24 hours after fever is gone (without medicine).

Symptoms:

  • Fever, cough, sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Fatigue, body aches, headache
  • Loss of taste or smell (less common now)

How it Spreads:

Airborne respiratory droplets and aerosols when an infected person breathes, talks, coughs, or sneezes.

Incubation Period:

Typically 2 to 6 days, but can be up to 14 days.

Contagious Period:

Most infectious from 1-2 days before symptoms start until 5-7 days after.

Return to School:

Fever-free for 24 hours (without medicine) AND overall symptoms have improved.

Symptoms:

  • Runny nose & cough
  • Fever & decreased appetite
  • Wheezing (in severe cases)

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets and direct contact with infected secretions or surfaces.

Incubation Period:

2 to 8 days.

Contagious Period:

3-8 days. Infants can shed virus for up to 4 weeks.

Return to School:

24 hours after fever is gone (without medicine).

Symptoms:

  • Harsh, barking cough (worse at night)
  • Hoarse voice
  • Noisy, high-pitched sound when inhaling (stridor)

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing, and contact with contaminated surfaces.

Incubation Period:

2 to 6 days.

Contagious Period:

For several days before symptoms start and for about a week after.

Return to School:

Fever-free for 24 hours and cough is manageable.

Symptoms:

  • Early: Mild cold-like symptoms
  • Later: Severe coughing fits followed by a high-pitched "whoop" sound
  • Vomiting during or after coughing fits

How it Spreads:

Airborne respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing. Very contagious.

Incubation Period:

7 to 10 days, but can be up to 21 days.

Contagious Period:

From onset of cold symptoms until 3 weeks after cough begins. Reduced to 5 days after starting effective antibiotics.

Return to School:

After completing 5 days of antibiotics.

Symptoms:

  • Severe sore throat
  • Pain swallowing
  • Fever & red tonsils

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing.

Incubation Period:

2 to 5 days.

Contagious Period:

Until 12-24 hours after starting antibiotics.

Return to School:

12-24 hours after first antibiotic dose & fever-free.

Symptoms:

  • Fever and chills
  • Productive cough (may have colored mucus)
  • Rapid breathing or difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain, fatigue

How it Spreads:

Caused by various bacteria and viruses. Spreads via respiratory droplets. Not as contagious as flu or cold.

Incubation Period:

Varies widely depending on the cause (e.g., 1-3 days for bacterial, longer for viral).

Contagious Period:

Varies. For bacterial pneumonia, usually not contagious after 24-48 hours on antibiotics.

Return to School:

Fever-free for 24 hours (without medicine) and feeling well enough to participate.

Symptoms:

  • Persistent, dry cough (often worse at night)
  • Low-grade fever & headache
  • Fatigue and general feeling of being unwell

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing, often through close contact.

Incubation Period:

1 to 4 weeks (long incubation period).

Contagious Period:

Can be contagious for days before symptoms and shed bacteria for weeks.

Return to School:

24 hours after starting effective antibiotics & fever-free.

Gastrointestinal (GI) Illnesses

Common Causes:

Norovirus (most common), Rotavirus (less common due to vaccine).

Symptoms:

  • Watery diarrhea, vomiting
  • Stomach cramps, low-grade fever

How it Spreads:

Fecal-oral route, contaminated food/water, surfaces, aerosolized vomit (Norovirus).

Incubation Period:

12 hours to 3 days, depending on the virus.

Contagious Period:

Most contagious during illness and for a few days after. Can be shed in stool for weeks.

Return to School:

48 hours after last episode of vomiting or diarrhea.

Common Causes:

Salmonella, E. coli, Campylobacter.

Symptoms:

  • Diarrhea (can be bloody), vomiting
  • Fever, severe stomach cramps

How it Spreads:

Ingesting contaminated food (especially undercooked meat/eggs) or water; contact with infected animals.

Incubation Period:

Varies widely, from hours to several days.

Contagious Period:

Can be contagious for as long as bacteria are shed in the stool (days to weeks).

Return to School:

48 hours after last episode of vomiting or diarrhea, and after consulting a doctor.

Symptoms:

  • Fever, fatigue, nausea, stomach pain
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes), dark urine
  • Many young children have no symptoms

How it Spreads:

Fecal-oral route, by ingesting contaminated food or water.

Incubation Period:

Average of 28 days (range: 15-50 days).

Contagious Period:

Most infectious in the 2 weeks before symptoms appear. Can last for a week after symptoms start.

Return to School:

One week after onset of illness/jaundice, or as advised by the health department.

Symptoms:

  • Itchy anal or vaginal area, especially at night
  • Restlessness, irritability, and difficulty sleeping
  • Occasional stomach pain or nausea

How it Spreads:

Fecal-oral route. Microscopic eggs are ingested after contact with contaminated fingers, bedding, clothing, or surfaces.

Incubation Period:

1 to 2 months from ingesting eggs to worms maturing and laying new eggs.

Contagious Period:

As long as worms are present. Eggs can survive on surfaces for 2-3 weeks.

Return to School:

No exclusion needed. Can return after the first dose of medication. Strict hand hygiene is crucial.

Rash / Skin Illnesses

Symptoms:

  • High fever, cough, runny nose
  • Red, watery eyes
  • Rash starting on face and spreading down

How it Spreads:

Airborne; virus can remain in the air for up to 2 hours.

Incubation Period:

7 to 21 days.

Contagious Period:

4 days before rash until 4 days after rash appears.

Return to School:

4 days after rash appears. (Unvaxxed contacts: 21 days).

Symptoms:

  • Fever & sore throat
  • Painful mouth sores
  • Rash/blisters on hands, feet, and sometimes buttocks

How it Spreads:

Fecal-oral route, contact with blister fluid or saliva, and respiratory droplets.

Incubation Period:

3 to 6 days.

Contagious Period:

Most contagious first week. Shed in stool for weeks.

Return to School:

Fever-free, feels well, and blisters are dry/healed.

Symptoms:

  • "Slapped cheek" rash on face
  • Lacy red rash on torso and limbs
  • Mild cold-like symptoms before rash

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing.

Incubation Period:

4 to 14 days.

Contagious Period:

Before the rash appears. Not contagious once rash is visible.

Return to School:

No exclusion needed once rash appears.

Symptoms:

  • Sudden high fever (3-5 days)
  • Fever ends, then a rash appears

How it Spreads:

Respiratory droplets from saliva, coughs, or sneezes.

Incubation Period:

About 9 to 10 days.

Contagious Period:

During the fever phase, before the rash appears.

Return to School:

24 hours after fever is gone (rash can be present).

Symptoms:

  • Red sores that rupture
  • Honey-colored crusts
  • Itching

How it Spreads:

Direct contact with sores or contaminated items (toys, towels).

Incubation Period:

1 to 10 days.

Contagious Period:

Until 24 hours after starting antibiotics or sores heal.

Return to School:

24 hours after starting antibiotics. Cover sores.

Symptoms:

  • Red, scaly, ring-shaped patch
  • Itching
  • Patch may be clear in the center

How it Spreads:

Direct skin-to-skin contact or with contaminated items (combs, hats).

Incubation Period:

4 to 14 days.

Contagious Period:

As long as the fungus is present on the skin and untreated.

Return to School:

After first treatment. Area should be kept covered.

Symptoms:

  • Red, itchy, watery eyes
  • Thick yellow/green discharge (Bacterial)
  • Watery discharge (Viral)

How it Spreads:

Direct contact with eye discharge or contaminated hands and objects.

Incubation Period:

Bacterial: 24-72 hours. Viral: 1-12 days.

Contagious Period:

As long as symptoms/discharge are present.

Return to School:

Bacterial: 24 hrs after antibiotics. Viral: When comfortable.
